## Methodology

Now that we're feeling confident with legal, compliance and a bit of branding, Taylor is free and clear to go bananas doing what he does best — examining Google search data to identify patterns/themes/SEO best practices that leave behind a beaten path of monthly search volume, keyword difficulty/competition and CPC (Cost-per-click).

SEO research typically starts by inferring the most common words or phrases your ideal clients (parents) type into Google that you would expect (as the domain expert) satisfy the intent of their search query (hiring an IEP advocate).

The first SEO metric we look for will always be monthly search volume as it is the most direct path to the 'highest amount of potential traffic' — this will begin expanding into an organized collection of main/parent/pillar topics we know people are searching in large volumes on a frequent basis.

We will expand more on this later, but for now just focus on the idea of embedding these keywords/phrases into our website to give Google a clear and complete understanding of who we are, the specific things we do, and where we're located.

## Seed Phrase Query: IEP Advocacy

**The result of our initial seed phrase query returned the following insights:**

> 1. Looking at the search volume data, "special education advocate" is the strongest term at 2,900 monthly searches, followed by "educational advocate" and "autism advocate" which both show solid demand.
> 2. The local intent angle with "iep advocate near me" at 1,000 searches is particularly valuable, and "autism advocate" stands out with a higher cost-per-click at $1.81, suggesting commercial intent. The local intent data confirms parents are searching geographically, so I should rely on Google Business Profile and location-specific content rather than embedding geography in the actual business name.
> 3. I'm also noticing that "BCBA" has zero search demand as a credential descriptor, so it shouldn't anchor the brand identity—it belongs in supporting materials instead. The naming strategy is becoming clearer: a brandable name paired with "IEP Advocacy" or "Special Education Advocacy" as the descriptor.
>
> (SEO results are often surprising and that's why it required a trained eye — most of the time a 'lack of search volume' you would expect to be higher relative to the rest of the data based on your domain expertise can be just as significant of a signal than an abundance of volume)

After we take a peek at the monthly search volume, the next thing we want to learn from Google is the related searches it suggests at the bottom of your seed phrase query + the People Also Ask section for questions that include an exact match of your high volume keywords.
