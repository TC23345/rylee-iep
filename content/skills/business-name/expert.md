# SEO Strategy

*Let parent search behavior set the structure of the name*

The naming decision starts with a practical constraint: parents do not search for credentials first. They search for the problem they are trying to solve. For this practice, the strongest terms in the research were the plain-language service descriptors: **IEP advocacy** and **IEP advocate**.

That gives the name a simple job. The legal/business name should include the descriptor parents already understand, while the brand word in front of it carries the emotional tone. Rylee's BCBA credential matters, but it belongs in supporting copy, the footer, the Google Business Profile, and the service pages where it builds trust without crowding the name.

## Naming rule from the SEO work

Use this structure:

> **Brandable word** + **IEP Advocacy** + **entity designator**

That structure keeps the name findable without making it generic. It also leaves room for the business to serve beyond one city, one disability category, or one credential identity.

## How to use this in the platform

- Use **IEP Advocacy** consistently in page titles, service descriptions, and the Google Business Profile.
- Use **BCBA-led** in the tagline and short description, not as the legal-name anchor.
- Use Illinois/local language in location copy and profile fields, not necessarily in the business name.
- Revisit keyword data later when the public site is built; this page is the naming decision record, not a full SEO plan.
