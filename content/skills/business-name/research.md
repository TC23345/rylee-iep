# Name Decision

*The working recommendation for the practice name*

The working name is:

> **Keystone IEP Advocacy LLC**

It follows the structure the SEO research pointed toward: a brandable lead word, the exact service descriptor parents search for, and the Illinois entity designator. The name also passes the plain-language test. A parent can hear it once on a phone call and understand the category of help being offered.

## Why this name works

| Piece | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Keystone | Brand hook: steady, structural, supportive, not overly clinical. |
| IEP Advocacy | Search and clarity: tells parents exactly what the practice helps with. |
| LLC | Filing/designator placeholder, subject to Illinois attorney/IDFPR verification. |

## What still needs verification

Illinois filing and professional-entity questions should be verified before the name is treated as final. Because Rylee is a licensed BCBA, the key question is whether the entity will be framed only as private-pay IEP advocacy or whether any behavior-analysis service language creates a professional-entity requirement.

<details>
<summary>Pre-filing checklist</summary>

- [ ] Search the exact name through the Illinois Secretary of State business entity search.
- [ ] Verify whether the entity should be LLC, PLLC, or another professional structure with an Illinois attorney/IDFPR.
- [ ] Check obvious trademark conflicts through USPTO search.
- [ ] Check domain availability and common social handles.
- [ ] Confirm the public tagline does not imply legal representation or clinical treatment.
- [ ] Save only status notes in the repo; do not store EIN or filing numbers here.

</details>

## Working tagline options

> **BCBA-led IEP advocacy for Illinois families.**

This is the cleanest short version. It places the credential where it builds trust without making the business name feel technical.

> **Board-certified behavior analysis meets special education advocacy.**

This is stronger as a longer profile or website sentence. It explains why Rylee is different, but it should be reviewed for scope clarity so the business is not accidentally presenting as an ABA therapy provider if that is not the intended service.
